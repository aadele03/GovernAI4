How to Use the
Governance Playbook

The Governance Playbook is your AI governance programme's structured project plan. It contains 35 tasks across six NIST AI RMF-aligned phases. Completing these tasks builds a documented, auditable programme that satisfies regulatory expectations and moves your Program Maturity score.

Step 01

Open the Governance Playbook

Click Governance Playbook in the left sidebar. The module opens with a split-panel layout — the six phases listed in the left rail and the tasks for the active phase displayed on the right.

Step 02

Understand the six phases

The Playbook is structured around the four NIST AI RMF functions — GOVERN, MAP, MEASURE, and MANAGE — broken into six sequential phases with 35 tasks in total. Work through them in order: Phase 1 first, Phase 6 last.

PHASE 1 · GOVERN
Establish AI Governance Foundations
Define your risk appetite, governance committee, roles and accountabilities, AI Use Policy, incident response procedures, and training requirements.
6 tasks · Must be completed first
PHASE 2 · MAP
Categorize Context & Use Cases
Identify all AI systems, document their business context and intended purpose, identify affected populations, assess human-AI interaction, classify risk, and map supply chains.
6 tasks · Links to AI Inventory
PHASE 3 · MAP
Identify & Analyze AI Risks
Complete structured risk assessments, evaluate bias and fairness, assess explainability, conduct vendor due diligence, document findings, and validate with the governance committee.
6 tasks · Links to Risk Assessment & VDD
PHASE 4 · MEASURE
Measure, Test & Analyze Performance
Define performance and fairness metrics, conduct bias testing, test reliability and failure modes, establish monitoring thresholds, and document measurement methodology.
6 tasks · Metrics and testing
PHASE 5 · MANAGE
Assign Controls & Treat Risks
Select treatment pathways for all Critical and High findings, define and assign controls, assess control effectiveness, establish a vendor remediation programme, and document formal risk acceptances.
5 tasks · Links to Risk Management
PHASE 6 · MANAGE
Monitor, Review & Operationalize
Establish review cadences, integrate AI risk into enterprise reporting, implement continuous monitoring, conduct programme reviews, prepare examination documentation, and publish governance status reports.
6 tasks · Ongoing operations
ℹ️
Complete phases in order. Each phase builds on the previous one. GOVERN must be complete before MAP tasks are meaningful. MAP must be done before MEASURE is actionable. You can navigate to any phase at any time but completing them in sequence produces the strongest governance programme.
Step 03

Navigate between phases

Click any phase in the left rail to load its tasks in the right panel. The rail shows the phase name, NIST function label, and a live task completion count. The active phase is highlighted with a coloured left border.

Step 04

Open and read a task

Click any task row to expand it. The expanded view shows the full task description, regulatory tags, required evidence items, an owner dropdown, a file upload zone, and the Attest button.

Step 05

Assign an owner

Every task should have a named owner — the individual accountable for completing it. Click the Owner dropdown inside the expanded task and select a person. Changes save automatically.

⚠️
Assign every task to a specific person before attesting. Unowned tasks are an accountability gap. Regulators and examiners expect named individuals responsible for each governance obligation. If the owner changes roles or leaves, update the assignment immediately.
Step 06

Upload evidence

Step 07

Attest a task complete

When a task has been completed — the work is done, the owner is assigned, and any required evidence is uploaded — click Attest complete. The task checkbox turns orange and the phase completion count increments.

⚠️
Attest only when the work is genuinely done. Attestation is a formal governance declaration. Attesting a task before the underlying work is complete — uploading a blank template as evidence, or attesting without an owner — creates a false governance record that regulators can challenge.
Step 08

Mark a phase complete

Once all tasks in a phase are attested and all required evidence is uploaded, a Mark Phase complete button appears at the bottom of the task list. Click it to formally close the phase and unlock the completion banner.

ℹ️
After marking a phase complete, a completion banner appears with a button to continue to the next phase. You can navigate freely between phases at any time — completing a phase does not lock you out of it. You can still update owners and add evidence after completion.
Step 09

Tasks that auto-complete

Five Playbook tasks are auto-completed when you take actions in other GovernAI modules. You do not need to attest these manually — they check themselves when the corresponding work is done.

TaskPhaseAuto-completes when…
m2a — Complete structured risk assessments MAP 2 You save a completed Risk Assessment for any High or Medium risk system in the Risk Assessment Engine
m2d — Review AI vendor risk MAP 2 You save a completed Vendor Due Diligence assessment in the VDD module
ms1 — Define performance metrics MEASURE You save a completed Risk Assessment (the dimension scores establish baseline performance metrics)
mg1a — Select treatment pathways MANAGE 1 You assign a treatment pathway (Mitigate, Accept, Transfer, or Avoid) to a finding in Risk Management
mg1b — Define and assign controls MANAGE 1 You assign a named owner to a finding in Risk Management
💡
Check the Playbook after completing other modules. After running a Risk Assessment, saving a VDD, or triaging findings, navigate to the Playbook to see which tasks have auto-completed. These updates move your overall completion percentage and Program Maturity score automatically.
