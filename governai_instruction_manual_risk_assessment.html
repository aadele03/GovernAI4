Instruction Manual · Risk Assessment Engine

How to Perform
a Risk Assessment

The Risk Assessment Engine converts a registered AI system from Provisional into a formally scored record. It produces an overall risk score, a risk tier, and automatically generates governance findings that flow directly to Risk Management.

⚖️ 4-step wizard 4 context questions 6 scoring dimensions 🛡 NIST AI RMF · MAP 2 · MEASURE ⏱ ~12 minutes
Steps
  • 01Open the Risk Assessment module
  • 02Start a new assessment & select a system
  • 03Answer the four context questions
  • 04Score the six risk dimensions
  • 05Review results & save
  • 06What happens automatically
  • 07Next steps
Step 01

Open the Risk Assessment module

Click Risk Assessment in the left sidebar. The module lists every registered AI system and its current assessment status. A system registered but not yet assessed shows as Provisional.

Step 02

Start a new assessment & select a system

Click + New assessment. The 4-step wizard opens on Step 1. Click the system you want to assess — it highlights with an orange border. Then click Next.

Step 03

Answer the four context questions

Step 2 shows four questions about how this system is used. Your answers shape how the scoring engine weights each dimension. Select one option per question — all four must be answered before you can continue.

QuestionHow to answer
1. Primary purpose Select how the system's output is actually used day-to-day — not how it was designed. If a human uses it to inform a decision, that is "supports operational processes." If it drives an automated outcome, select the first option.
2. Who is affected Think broadly. If even some of the affected parties are external individuals — customers, applicants, patients — select that option. Do not under-scope.
3. Consequence of error Consider the worst realistic scenario if this system produces a wrong output. Think about regulatory exposure, financial impact on individuals, and safety implications.
4. Formal validation Answer based on what has actually been completed, not what is planned. If bias testing is scheduled but not done, select "No formal validation."
⚠️
Answer for today, not your plans. The assessment measures your actual current exposure — which is what regulators will inspect. Score what is true now.
Step 04

Score the six risk dimensions

Step 3 shows six risk dimensions, each with a slider from 1 (low risk) to 10 (high risk). Drag each slider to reflect where this system sits today. The final score is a weighted average across all six.

DimensionWeightScore high (7–10) when…
Decision impact 22% The system directly determines consequential outcomes for individuals — loan approvals, clinical decisions, hiring
Data sensitivity 20% The system processes PHI, financial data, biometrics, or other regulated personal data categories
Regulatory exposure 20% The system is directly subject to SR 11-7, HIPAA, FDA SaMD, ECOA, or EU AI Act obligations
Human oversight 15% The system is fully automated — outputs drive action with no human review before decisions are made
Third-party dependency 13% The vendor provides a black-box model with minimal documentation and no audit rights
Model transparency 10% Outputs cannot be explained — no feature importance, no audit trail, no way to reconstruct a decision
⚠️
Score what is true today, not what you plan to improve. If you intend to add human oversight next quarter but it is not in place yet, score Human oversight as high today. The assessment records your actual exposure.
Step 05

Review results & save

Step 4 shows your overall score, risk tier, and the governance findings automatically generated from your dimension scores. Review everything carefully, then click Save assessment.

Score rangeRisk tierWhat it means
1 – 3 Low Internal or support tools with limited personal data and strong oversight. Standard annual governance review cycle.
4 – 6 Medium Systems with moderate impact or data sensitivity. Formal controls required. Semi-annual review recommended.
7 – 10 High Consequential, regulated, or opaque systems. Immediate remediation of findings required. Quarterly review cycle.
ℹ️
Findings are generated automatically. You do not need to create them manually. Any dimension scoring above its threshold produces a finding that flows directly to Risk Management when you save.
Step 06

What happens automatically

When you click Save assessment, the record updates and four things happen across GovernAI without any further action needed.

✅
Status changes to Complete
The system row shows "Complete" with its score and risk tier. The provisional record is replaced by the formal assessment.
🛡
Findings flow to Risk Management
All auto-generated findings appear immediately in the Risk Management module, grouped under this system and ready for triage and ownership assignment.
📋
Two Playbook tasks auto-complete
MAP 2a (risk identification) and MEASURE 1 (metrics baseline) are marked complete in the Governance Playbook automatically.
📊
Dashboard maturity updates
The "AI Systems Assessed" metric increases and the Program Maturity score recalculates on the GRC Dashboard.
Step 07

Next steps

With the assessment saved, address the findings it generated and continue building out governance coverage for this system.

  • 1
    Triage findings in Risk Management. Navigate to Risk Management — your auto-generated findings are already there. For each finding: assign a treatment pathway (Mitigate, Accept, Transfer, or Avoid), assign a named owner, and set a remediation deadline.
  • 2
    Complete Vendor Due Diligence. If the vendor for this system has not yet been assessed, navigate to Vendor Due Diligence → find the vendor record → click "+ New assessment" → complete the 53-question framework.
  • 3
    Review the Governance Playbook. Navigate to the Playbook — MAP 2 and MEASURE 1 tasks have auto-completed. Check remaining open tasks in those phases and assign owners to any that are unassigned.
  • 4
    Return to the GRC Dashboard. Check your updated Program Maturity score and review any new Priority Alerts. High-risk assessments generate urgent alerts that require immediate attention.
