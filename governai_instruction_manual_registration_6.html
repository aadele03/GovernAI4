How to Register
a New AI System

The AI Inventory is the foundation of GovernAI. Every other module — Risk Assessment, Vendor Due Diligence, Risk Management — draws its data from systems registered here. A system not in the Inventory is invisible to governance.

📋 11 fields 🛡 NIST AI RMF · MAP 1.1 ⚡ 3 automatic effects ⏱ ~5 minutes

Step 01

Open the registration form

Click AI Inventory in the left sidebar, then click the orange + Register system button in the top-right corner of the controls bar. The registration form opens as a modal in the centre of the screen.

Step 02

System name, vendor & version

The form opens with four fields in the first two rows. Fill in the system's formal identity — use the exact name from your vendor contract.

FieldTagWhat to enter
System name Required The exact name from your vendor contract. Regulators match your records against the contract — do not use internal nicknames.
Vendor Required The vendor or provider name. For internally built systems enter your organisation's name.
Version Optional Current model or software version. Useful for tracking when upgrades change the risk profile.
Business unit Optional The department that owns and operates this system. Select from the dropdown (Not assigned, Credit Risk, Clinical Operations, Fraud Operations, Engineering, Compliance, Marketing, HR, Finance, Legal, Other).
Step 03

Risk owner, dates & use case

These fields establish accountability, set the governance review calendar, and describe what the system does.

FieldTagWhat to enter
Risk owner Critical A named individual — not a team or department. Select from the dropdown (Chief Risk Officer, CISO, Chief Privacy Officer, Head of Compliance, and others). When someone leaves this role, update this field immediately.
Contract renewal Optional The date your vendor contract renews. GovernAI surfaces upcoming renewals in Dashboard alerts so you can renegotiate governance terms before automatic renewal.
Review cycle Optional How frequently governance should be reviewed. Default is Annual. High-risk systems should be reviewed Quarterly or Semi-annually.
Use case Optional One or two sentences describing what this system does and what decision or output it produces. Write it for an auditor who has never seen this system.
Step 04

Regulatory flags

Check every regulation that applies to how this system operates, the data it uses, and the decisions it makes. When in doubt — flag it. A missing flag is a compliance gap.

FlagApply when…
ECOA / Fair lendingSystem makes or supports credit, lending, or housing decisions
SR 11-7Any model used at a bank or financial institution for risk measurement or decisioning
HIPAASystem processes Protected Health Information (PHI)
FDA SaMDAI used in clinical diagnosis, treatment, or medical decision support
EU AI Act — high riskAI in HR, credit, education, or law enforcement targeting EU persons
EU AI Act — limited riskAI generating consumer-facing content or interacting with EU persons
GDPR / CPRAProcesses personal data of EU residents or California consumers
NYC Local Law 144Automated employment decision tool screening NYC candidates
Illinois AEIAAI used in employment decisions in Illinois
⚠️
It is far better to have an extra flag you later formally decide does not apply, than to miss one that does. Consult your compliance team if uncertain about any regulation.
Step 05

Data types & human oversight

Select every category of data this system processes. Then confirm the human oversight checkbox — this has the single largest impact on the Risk Assessment score.

Data typeApply when…
PIIName, address, SSN, email, phone, date of birth, IP address, device IDs
PHIMedical records, diagnoses, prescriptions, insurance claims
FinancialAccount numbers, credit scores, transaction history, income data
BiometricFacial recognition, fingerprints, voice prints, retinal scans
Source code / IPProprietary code, trade secrets, technical intellectual property
⚠️
Human oversight is checked by default. Uncheck it only if the system truly operates autonomously with no human review before decisions are acted upon. The Risk Assessment Engine weights this field at 18% of the total score.
Step 06

Save & what happens automatically

Click the orange Save button. The modal closes and the new system appears immediately in the Inventory list. Three records are created automatically across other modules.

⚖️
Risk Assessment record
A provisional record is created in the Risk Assessment Engine, queued for formal scoring.
🤝
Vendor Due Diligence record
A VDD record is created for this vendor, linked automatically to any existing assessment.
📊
Dashboard updated
AI Systems count increases and the department heatmap updates immediately.
Step 07

Next steps

Registration creates the governance foundation. Complete these steps to activate full coverage for this system.

  • 1
    Run the formal Risk Assessment. Go to Risk Assessment → click "+ New assessment" → select this system → complete the 4-step wizard. This produces a formal risk score and auto-generates governance findings.
  • 2
    Complete Vendor Due Diligence. Go to Vendor Due Diligence → find the new vendor record → click "+ New assessment" → complete the 53-question framework.
  • 3
    Complete MAP 1 Playbook tasks. Go to Governance Playbook → MAP 1 phase → complete the tasks covering system context, use case documentation, and stakeholder impact.
  • 4
    Check the GRC Dashboard. Review your updated Program Maturity score and any new Priority Alerts generated by this registration.
