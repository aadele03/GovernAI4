Instruction Manual · Vendor Due Diligence

How to Perform a
Vendor Due Diligence
Assessment

Vendor Due Diligence (VDD) evaluates the AI governance posture of every vendor supplying AI systems to your organisation. A VDD record is created automatically when you register a system. This manual walks you through completing the formal assessment.

🤝 4-step wizard 53 questions across 6 domains 9-item contract checklist 🛡 SR 11-7 · EU AI Act Art. 28 ⏱ ~20 minutes
Steps
  • 01Open the Vendor Due Diligence module
  • 02Start a new assessment & select a vendor
  • 03Score the six governance domains
  • 04Complete the contract provisions checklist
  • 05Review results & save
  • 06What happens automatically
  • 07Next steps
Step 01

Open the Vendor Due Diligence module

Click Vendor Due Diligence in the left sidebar. The module lists all vendors linked to registered AI systems. A vendor not yet assessed shows as Pending. When you registered the Customer Churn Predictor, a Pending VDD record was created automatically for that vendor.

Step 02

Start a new assessment & select a vendor

Click + New assessment. The 4-step wizard opens. Step 1 shows all vendors — click the vendor you want to assess to select it, then click Next.

💾
You can save a draft at any time. The wizard saves your progress automatically as you move between steps. If you need to gather more information from the vendor, exit and return later — your answers will be waiting.
Step 03

Score the six governance domains

Step 2 presents 53 Yes/No questions across six governance domains. Answer Yes only when you have documented evidence. Each domain is scored as a percentage of Yes answers and feeds into the overall vendor score.

Model Governance
8 questions · Model inventory, MDLC, validation, change management, monitoring
Data Handling & Residency
9 questions · DPA/BAA, training data prohibition, sub-processors, encryption, deletion
Security
10 questions · SOC 2, pen testing, vulnerability management, access controls, incident response
Transparency & Explainability
8 questions · Model cards, output explainability, bias disclosure, performance reporting
Business Continuity
8 questions · RTO/RPO, DR testing, exit planning, contractual SLAs, incident history
Fairness & Bias Testing
10 questions · Bias methodology, protected classes, third-party audits, remediation process
⚠️
Only select Yes when you have documented evidence. If the vendor verbally confirms something but has not provided documentation, select No. The assessment must be based on what you can substantiate — not what you have been told.
Step 04

Complete the contract provisions checklist

Step 3 presents nine contract provisions that should appear in every AI vendor contract. Check each provision that is confirmed in your current contract. Seven are marked Required — missing any will generate a Critical gap finding.

ProvisionStatusWhat it means
Training data prohibitionRequiredVendor may not use your customer data to train or fine-tune their AI models without your explicit written consent
Model change notificationRequiredVendor must give you at least 30 days advance notice of any material changes to the AI model
Explainability obligationRequiredVendor must provide an explanation of any AI output on request — critical for adverse action compliance
IP ownership of outputsRequiredYour organisation owns all outputs generated using your customer data
Audit rightsRequiredYou have the right to audit the vendor's AI practices, controls, and model documentation
Sub-processor disclosureRequiredVendor must disclose all sub-processors involved in AI delivery — cloud GPU providers, vector databases, third-party APIs
Data deletion / exit termsRequiredVendor must delete all customer data within 30 days of contract termination, including all copies and training artefacts
Bias audit requirementOptionalVendor is contractually required to conduct an annual third-party bias audit
SLA with financial penaltiesOptionalDefined uptime and performance SLAs with financial remedies for breach
⚠️
Use this checklist at contract renewal. Any Required provision not present in your current contract should become a non-negotiable addition at the next renewal. Missing Required provisions generate Critical findings in Risk Management.
Step 05

Review results & save

Step 4 shows your overall vendor score (0–100), the verdict, domain-by-domain breakdown, and any contract gaps identified. Review everything carefully, then click Save assessment.

Score rangeVerdictWhat it means
75 – 100 Approved Vendor meets governance requirements. Standard annual review. Minor gaps should be addressed at next contract renewal.
50 – 74 Conditional Significant gaps identified. Vendor may continue operating but gaps must be remediated with a documented timeline. Enhanced monitoring required.
0 – 49 Flagged Vendor fails governance threshold. Escalation to risk committee required. Consider suspension of new use cases pending remediation.
ℹ️
Score formula: Domain scores account for 70% of the total. Contract provision coverage accounts for 30%. A vendor can have strong domain scores but still score Conditional if Required contract provisions are missing.
Step 06

What happens automatically

When you click Save assessment, the vendor record updates and four things happen across GovernAI without further action needed.

✅
Vendor status updated
The vendor row shows the verdict (Approved, Conditional, or Flagged) with the numerical score. The Pending status is replaced by the formal assessment result.
🛡
Gap findings flow to Risk Management
Any domain scoring below threshold and any missing Required contract provisions generate findings automatically in Risk Management, ready for triage and ownership assignment.
📋
Playbook task auto-completes
The MAP 2 Vendor due diligence task in the Governance Playbook is automatically marked complete for the linked AI system.
📊
Dashboard vendor coverage updates
The "Vendor Coverage" metric on the GRC Dashboard increases and the Program Maturity score recalculates. Flagged vendors generate Priority Alerts.
Step 07

Next steps

With the VDD assessment saved, address the gap findings it generated and use the results to strengthen your vendor contract and monitoring approach.

  • 1
    Triage gap findings in Risk Management. Navigate to Risk Management — your auto-generated findings are already there. For each gap finding: assign a treatment pathway, name an owner, and set a remediation deadline. Contract gaps should be assigned to your legal or procurement team for resolution at next renewal.
  • 2
    Use the contract checklist at renewal. Any Required provision missing from your current contract should be a non-negotiable addition. Export the VDD results and share with your legal team before entering renewal negotiations.
  • 3
    Check the Governance Playbook. Navigate to the Playbook — the MAP 2 Vendor Due Diligence task has auto-completed. Review any remaining MAP 2 tasks and check whether MEASURE phase tasks need attention.
  • 4
    Return to the GRC Dashboard. Check your updated Program Maturity score and review any new Priority Alerts. A Flagged vendor generates an urgent alert that requires escalation to the risk committee.
