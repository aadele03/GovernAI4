Instruction Manual · Risk Management

How to Use the
Risk Management
Module

Risk Management is where governance becomes action. Findings flow here automatically from Risk Assessment and Vendor Due Diligence. Your job is to triage each finding, assign a treatment pathway and owner, track remediation to completion, and formally close or accept each risk.

Step 01

Open the Risk Management module

Click Risk Management in the left sidebar. The module shows a summary bar at the top with your total findings count broken down by status, then a grouped list of all findings below. Findings are grouped by AI system with the highest-risk systems appearing first.

ℹ️
Findings arrive here automatically. Every time you complete a Risk Assessment or Vendor Due Diligence assessment, the findings generated flow directly into this module. You do not need to create them — your job is to triage and resolve them.
Step 02

Read the findings list

Each row in the list shows one finding. The left border colour indicates severity at a glance. Use the filter pills to focus on what needs immediate action — start with Critical and Overdue.

SeverityWhat it meansPriority
CriticalImmediate risk to individuals, regulatory exposure, or material control failureAddress first
HighSignificant gap requiring formal remediation within 30 daysUrgent
MediumNotable gap to be addressed within the current review cycleStandard
LowMinor issue or enhancement — monitor and address at next reviewMonitor
Step 03

Open a finding & review the detail panel

Click any row to open the full detail panel on the right side of the screen. The detail panel shows everything about the finding and is where all triage actions are taken.

Step 04

Assign a treatment pathway

In the detail panel, click one of the four treatment pathway cards to assign how this risk will be handled. The selected pathway determines what actions are required and how the finding is ultimately closed.

🔧 Mitigate
Implement one or more controls to reduce the likelihood or impact of the risk. This is the most common pathway for Critical and High findings. Requires documented evidence of the control implemented.
✅ Accept
Formally accept the residual risk with a documented rationale and review period. Only appropriate for Low and some Medium risks that fall within your risk appetite. Requires formal acceptance record (see Step 7).
🔄 Transfer
Shift the risk to a third party through insurance, contract indemnification, or contractual obligation. The risk is not eliminated but your organisation's exposure is reduced. Requires documented transfer instrument.
🚫 Avoid
Eliminate the activity or system generating the risk entirely. Used when a risk cannot be brought within appetite and no other pathway is viable. Requires formal decommission or discontinuation decision.
⚠️
Critical findings should almost always be Mitigated. Accepting a Critical finding requires documented board or executive approval and is subject to examiner scrutiny. Avoid and Transfer are rarely appropriate for Critical findings unless the system itself is being discontinued.
Step 05

Assign an owner & set a deadline

Every finding must have a named owner and a remediation deadline. These fields appear in the Assignment section of the detail panel. Changes save automatically — there is no separate Save button for these fields.

FieldWhat to enter
Owner A named individual accountable for remediating this finding — not a team or department. Select from the same owner list used in the AI Inventory. Update immediately if the owner changes roles.
Deadline The date by which remediation must be complete. Critical findings: 30 days. High: 60 days. Medium: within the current review cycle. Low: next annual review. Findings past their deadline show as Overdue in the list and on the Dashboard.
Notes Add a timestamped entry every time you take an action. Include: what was done, who was contacted, what evidence was gathered, and what the next action is. These notes form your audit trail for this finding.
Step 06

Advance the finding through the workflow

Every finding moves through three statuses: Open → In Progress → Resolved. Use the action button at the bottom of the detail panel to advance status. Each advance is logged automatically in the audit trail.

🔴
Open
Finding identified. Triage required — assign pathway, owner, and deadline.
→
🟡
In Progress
Remediation is actively underway. Add notes as work progresses.
→
🟢
Resolved
Control implemented and verified. Attach evidence file before resolving.
⚠️
Attach evidence before marking Resolved. Use the Attach file button in the detail panel to upload the document proving the control was implemented — a policy document, test result, vendor confirmation, screenshot, or meeting minutes. Resolving without evidence creates an audit gap.
Step 07

Formal risk acceptance

If a finding has been assigned the Accept pathway and the status is Open, a blue Formally accept risk button appears alongside the standard action buttons. This creates a permanent acceptance record in the audit trail.

⚠️
Risk acceptance is not "ignore it." It is a documented, deliberate decision that this risk falls within your organisation's risk appetite. Before clicking Formally accept risk, ensure you have: (1) documented the business rationale in the Notes field, (2) confirmed the decision with the appropriate approver, and (3) set a review date to reassess the accepted risk.
Step 08

Adding a finding manually

Most findings arrive automatically from Risk Assessment and Vendor Due Diligence. However, you can also add findings manually — for example, from an internal audit, a regulatory examination, or a self-identified control gap. Click + Add finding in the controls bar.

FieldWhat to enter
Finding titleA concise description of the risk or gap. Write it so anyone reading the list can understand what the issue is without opening the detail panel.
DescriptionFull context: what was observed, why it matters, what the regulatory or operational impact is, and what evidence was found.
SeverityCritical / High / Medium / Low. Match to your risk appetite framework — apply the same standards as the automated findings from Risk Assessment.
AI systemLink to the specific registered AI system this finding applies to. The finding will then appear under that system's group in the list.
TreatmentMitigate, Accept, Transfer, or Avoid. Can be changed later in the detail panel.
OwnerThe individual accountable for remediation. Can be updated in the detail panel.
Step 09

Next steps

With findings triaged and in motion, maintain momentum by checking Risk Management regularly and keeping the Dashboard up to date.

  • 1
    Work Critical and Overdue findings first. Use the filter pills at the top of the list to isolate these. Every day a Critical finding sits Open without an owner and deadline is a regulatory exposure. Assign and start immediately.
  • 2
    Add notes every time you take an action. The Notes field in the detail panel is your evidence of diligence. "Contacted vendor 12 May, model card requested, response expected by 26 May" is the kind of entry that satisfies examiners. Bare status changes without notes do not.
  • 3
    Attach evidence before marking Resolved. Upload the document, policy, test result, or confirmation that proves the control was implemented. Use the Attach file button in the detail panel. The file is stored with the finding permanently.
  • 4
    Review the Governance Playbook. Resolving findings in Risk Management automatically advances some MANAGE phase Playbook tasks. Navigate to the Playbook to check which tasks are now complete and what remains open.
  • 5
    Monitor the GRC Dashboard. The Critical Findings Resolved metric accounts for 30% of your Program Maturity score. As you resolve findings, the score increases. Check the Board Pack report before any governance committee meeting to see the latest position.
